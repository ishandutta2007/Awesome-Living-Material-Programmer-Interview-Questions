# Additional Resources

A short list of resources for going deeper on engineered living materials. (Not exhaustive — PRs welcome.) This is a young, fast-moving field spanning synthetic biology and materials science — treat any specific technology readiness or performance claim as a snapshot in time and verify against current literature before an interview.

## Foundational Reading
- Nguyen, Gao & Lu, "Engineered living materials: prospects and challenges for using biological systems to direct the assembly of smart materials" (Materials Today, 2018) — a widely-cited foundational review of the field's core concepts and open challenges.
- Rodrigo-Navarro et al., "Engineered living biomaterials" (Nature Reviews Materials, 2021) — a comprehensive, technically rigorous review covering the field's major application areas and design strategies.
- Gilbert & Ellis, "Biological Engineered Living Materials: Growing Functional Materials with Genetically Programmable Properties" (ACS Synthetic Biology) — good grounding specifically on the genetic-programming side of the field.
- Companion **Synthetic Biology Designer** repo — read first if genetic circuit design, the DBTL cycle, and biosafety fundamentals aren't already familiar, since this repo builds directly on them.
- Companion **Biomimetic Structural Engineer** repo — read first if structural mechanics and materials characterization fundamentals aren't already familiar.

## Key Application Areas (good to be conversationally familiar with)
- Self-healing concrete using microbially-induced calcium carbonate precipitation (MICP) — one of the most mature, widely-cited application areas discussed in this field.
- Mycelium-based materials for packaging, construction, and other structural applications — a comparatively accessible entry point given fungi's substantial natural material-relevant capability requiring less extensive genetic engineering.
- Living biofilm-based and bioprinted living material systems explored for sensing, environmental remediation, and other responsive-material applications.

## Communities & Discussion
- iGEM (International Genetically Engineered Machine) competition project archives frequently include engineered living materials-relevant projects, providing accessible, documented examples of real (including student-level) design attempts and their outcomes.
- r/synbio and relevant materials science discussion communities for broader discussion threads.
- Major synthetic biology and materials science conferences increasingly feature dedicated engineered living materials tracks/sessions.

## Companion Repos (adjacent, complementary content)
- **Synthetic Biology Designer** — genetic circuit design, DNA assembly, host/metabolic engineering, and biosafety fundamentals this repo builds directly on.
- **Biomimetic Structural Engineer** — structural mechanics, materials characterization, and computational design fundamentals relevant to the non-living scaffold/material engineering side of this work.
- **Computational Biologist** — relevant background on modeling and characterization methods sometimes applied to the biological side of engineered living material systems.

## Staying Current
- This field remains largely at the laboratory/demonstration stage — recent conference proceedings and journal publications are far more reliable sources of current technology readiness than any static reference, including this repo, and interviewers may reasonably expect candidates to be aware of this honest state of the field rather than overstating current commercial maturity.
- Genetic engineering tools, bioprinting technology, and scaffold materials science are all advancing quickly and somewhat independently — following recent literature across all three areas (rather than only one) is valuable given how genuinely interdisciplinary this field is.
